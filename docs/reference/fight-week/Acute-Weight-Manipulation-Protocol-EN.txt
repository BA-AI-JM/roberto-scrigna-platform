Acute Weight Manipulation Protocol


Discontinue creatine supplementation 7 days before the weigh-in.

All foods consumed during fight week must be freshly prepared. Do not use deli meats, canned tuna, bread, salted rice cakes, canned or preserved foods, or foods whose preparation involves added salt (including bread, cottage cheese, certain types of rice cakes, Grana Padano, butter, cheese in general, and ready-made burgers). Check food labels: salt must not appear among the ingredients.

Recommended supplementation during fight week:

Multivitamin: 2 tablets per day, taken with a meal.

Slow-release vitamin C (C-Tard): 3 g per day, divided into 1 g in the morning, 1 g in the afternoon, and 1 g in the evening.

High-dose omega-3 providing EPA + DHA: 4 softgels per day, taken with a meal.

Vitamin D (Dibase) 10,000 IU/mL: 5-10 drops per day, taken with a meal.

Probiotics: Probactiol Duo (Metagenics) or VSL#3: 2 tablets per day from day -7 through match day, inclusive.

Optional:
Glutamine: 10 g per day, divided into two 5 g doses taken with meals.
Zinc (ZincoDyn - Metagenics): 2 tablets per day, in the morning before breakfast.
N-acetylcysteine (NAC): 600 mg twice per day (antioxidant, mucolytic, and hepatoprotective support).




Daily water and salt intake

Drink water in “boluses”, meaning relatively large amounts at regular, closely spaced intervals. For example, drink one 200-250 mL glass of water every 20 minutes, all at once.
Alternatively, drink approximately 300 mL of water every 30 minutes, equivalent to about one and a half glasses.
Water intake must still be distributed evenly throughout the day. Drinking several litres too close together is strongly discouraged, so make an effort to drink steadily and consistently across the day.
It is preferable to stop drinking a few hours before going to bed, to limit sleep disruption.

For salt, the number indicates the total grams to use during the day. When the number is 0, no salt is allowed and everything consumed must be unsalted and contain no added salt.

If you train and SWEAT during the day, check your body weight BEFORE AND AFTER training and increase your daily water intake by exactly the amount of body weight lost.

During fight week, if you still have carbohydrates available beyond those contained in yogurt, chocolate, avocado, and the two servings of fruit that will remain in the plan, place all of them in the PRE-WORKOUT window.

Pre-workout guidance against cramps: use spicy foods capable of activating TRPA1 receptors, such as chilli pepper, fresh ginger, black pepper, Sichuan pepper, wasabi, hot curry, mustard, and hot mustard.
During a cramp, you may take something containing acetic acid, such as pickle brine, a small sachet of Heinz Yellow Mustard, or a sachet of white wine vinegar.
Example: lemon-and-ginger Haribo + Heinz mustard sachet.

Carbohydrate mouth rinse: prepare 500 mL of water containing 30-40 g of total carbohydrate from sugar or maltodextrin. Take it to training and, every 5-10 minutes, rinse your mouth with 25-30 mL for 5-10 seconds, moving the liquid throughout the mouth. Spit the liquid out to avoid ingesting the carbohydrates.

Day before the weigh-in
I recommend keeping one glass of water available before going to bed, approximately 100-200 mL saved from your daily allowance. This may reduce thirst during the night and allow you to prepare chamomile tea or another relaxing herbal tea.
You may also buy chewing gum to use during the day to reduce the sensation of thirst.
In some cases, we may consider using a natural diuretic such as hawkweed or dandelion extract, or an equivalent product, together with support for bowel regularity such as 12 Erbe or a specific herbal tea.
If your body weight is already very low on the evening before the weigh-in, or if you have already completed a dehydration round using sauna plus blankets or a hot bath plus blankets, we may consider allowing something light before sleep. Options include:
a sleep-promoting herbal tea, such as chamomile tea;


frozen yogurt;


a small amount of ice cream.


The total weight of these foods should not exceed 100-250 g, depending on your specific situation.
Always message me so that we can decide together whether you can afford it at that point or whether it is better to avoid it.

Weigh-in day
We will determine the cutting strategy and method according to:
the weigh-in time, for example late morning, lunchtime, or afternoon;


the target body weight;


the equipment available, including a sauna, bath, sweat suits, thermal sleeping bag, portable sauna units, and the number of towels available.


Weight-cutting work
We will use 15-30-minute cycles consisting of:
an active phase: light movement, sauna, or hot bath;


a passive phase: sweating under blankets and towels while resting.


The cycles may be repeated until the target body weight is reached, while continuously monitoring body weight, sweating, alertness, and vital signs.

Important: DO NOT STAND UP QUICKLY while completing the weight-cutting cycles. You may faint due to orthostatic hypotension, because blood flow to the brain can temporarily decrease.


WEIGH-IN: Immediate rehydration
IMMEDIATELY AFTER THE WEIGH-IN: consume 500 mL of WATER + REIDRAX, IDRAVITA, SiS GO Electrolyte Tablets, or LIQUID I.V. immediately. Prepare at least 70% of the amount of fluid corresponding to the body weight you cut, divided into small 500 mL bottles.
Wait 5-10 minutes and then consume another 250 mL immediately.

After these first two boluses, continue consuming 250 mL of water plus Reidrax every 15 minutes. This means drinking 1.5-2 L during the first hour at most, structured as 500 mL immediately, 250 mL after 5-10 minutes, and then 250 mL approximately every 20 minutes.

0-1 h post-weigh-in: 1.5-2 L REIDRAX


Suggested intake schedule during the first 0-1 h post-weigh-in

0-10 min: 250-500 mL
10-20 min: 250 mL
20-30 min: 250 mL
30-45 min: 250 mL
45-60 min: 250 mL

After the first hour, continue drinking approximately 1-1.5 L of fluid per hour for 4-6 hours, at a rate of 250 mL every 15-20 minutes.

You will need to replace 150% of the body weight lost through dehydration. For example, a 3 kg dehydration loss requires at least 4.5 L of fluid replacement.




FIRST MEAL AFTER THE WEIGH-IN:
During the first hour after the weigh-in, it is preferable to focus only on drinking, or to eat small amounts of very easily digested carbohydrates such as rice cream, pudding, banana bread, puffed cereals, rusks or rice cakes, honey or jam, fruit, and fruit purées.

Once the first hour has passed and some fluid has been replaced, prioritise easily digested, cooked or dry, non-whole-grain carbohydrate foods such as white rice, white pasta, white bread, rice cakes, potatoes, or salty snacks, together with moderate amounts of simple carbohydrates such as honey, jam, or fruit juice. These may be combined with salty lean protein, such as 100 g sliced chicken, turkey, or bresaola, or with whey protein. No vegetables.
At the end of the meal, take 4-6 g creatine, dissolved thoroughly in warm water, together with Metadigest digestive enzymes: 1-2 tablets with each meal.

0-1 h after the weigh-in: if you dehydrated, LIQUIDS ONLY.

1-2 h post-weigh-in: simple carbohydrates + complex carbohydrates + protein + creatine + digestive enzymes.

EXAMPLES:
80 g rice cakes + 30 g honey + whey protein isolate shake
120 g white bread + 30 g jam + 80 g bresaola or 30 g whey isolate
100 g chocolate rice pudding + 30 g honey + 20 g whey protein
100 g white rice + 100 g sliced chicken + 250 mL fruit juice
100 g pasta with tomato sauce + 1 can of tuna in water + 1 Coca-Cola
200 g 0% Greek yogurt + 30 g honey + 70 g cornflakes + 200 mL fruit juice
200 mL skimmed milk + 20 g whey isolate + 30 g Nesquik + 70 g Coco Pops

MACROS: 20-30 g protein; 1-2 g carbohydrate per kg body weight, approximately 70-160 g; low fat; low fibre.


SUBSEQUENT MEALS:
They should be similar to the previous meal and consumed every 2-3 hours, depending on your sense of satiety and fullness.
Use only foods that you regularly consumed during the fight camp.
Consume no more than 2-3 servings of fruit per day and only small amounts of vegetables, preferably cooked.
Use plenty of salt. Take 5 g creatine at regular intervals until you reach a total of 20-30 g creatine per day, both on weigh-in day and on match day.
Take at least 2 Metadigest digestive-enzyme tablets with each meal.

2-3 h post-weigh-in: complex carbohydrates + lean protein + a small amount of fat, 5-10 g + a small amount of soft vegetables + REIDRAX, or coconut water if Reidrax has been finished + creatine + digestive enzymes.

EXAMPLES:
150-200 g pasta + 200 g tomato + 80 g bresaola + 20 g Grana Padano
150-200 g rice + 120 g white meat + 30 g Parmesan
1 kg potatoes + 150 g white fish + 1 tablespoon oil

From 3 h onward: complex carbohydrates and simple carbohydrates at approximately 1.2 g/kg/h + lean protein + creatine + digestive enzymes. With meals, take the planned supplements + BIOCHETASI + VSL#3.

EXAMPLES: 200 g Greek yogurt + 20 g honey + 100 g cornflakes + 1 banana
OR: 150 g white bread + 100 g sliced turkey + 125 g blueberries
OR: 150 g rice + 120 g white meat + 200 g spinach + 20 g Grana Padano

EAT 4-6 MEALS PER DAY. If you feel excessively full, stop eating, take digestive enzymes, and perform light movement such as walking. If your stomach feels closed or you cannot tolerate solid food, consume liquid calories such as milkshakes, shakes made with milk, instant oats, whey protein and honey, rice cream, semolina porridge, or rice pudding.

AT THE END OF EACH MEAL: take creatine monohydrate, digestive enzymes, and approximately half a teaspoon of table salt, around 2 g, swallowed separately as a “shot”.

FLUIDS: during the first 4-6 hours, depending on how much body weight was cut, continue drinking at least 1 L per hour, approximately 250 mL every 15 minutes.

REHYDRATION AND REFUELLING DRINK: for each litre of water, dissolve half the recommended dose of Reidrax, Dicodral, or Idravita and add 40-60 g per litre of Vitargo or maltodextrin.


MATCH DAY:

The number of meals will depend on the time at which you compete. If the match is early in the afternoon, approximately 15:00-18:00, you may only need breakfast, lunch, and possibly a small liquid snack.
If the match is later in the evening, approximately 18:00-23:00, you will probably also need a snack and possibly dinner.
As the match approaches, reduce both the portion sizes, particularly the amount of carbohydrate, and possibly remove protein and fibre completely from the final meal. You may also need to switch exclusively to liquid calories.
Two examples follow:

BREAKFAST: 300 mL plant-based milk + 20-30 g whey protein + 100 g Cheerios + 250 mL fruit juice. Take creatine, digestive enzymes, and table salt separately at the end of the meal.

LUNCH: 150 g white pasta or white rice + 80-100 g bresaola + 200 g beetroot or spinach + 1 teaspoon oil + 1 regular Coca-Cola containing sugar. Take creatine, digestive enzymes, and table salt separately at the end of the meal.

SNACK FOR AN EARLY MATCH: 20-30 g rice cakes + 40-50 g jam + 250 mL fruit juice. Alternatively: 250 mL milk + 20-40 g Nesquik + 30-50 g puffed cereal.

Take creatine and digestive enzymes separately at the end of the meal.

SNACK FOR A LATE MATCH: 100 g white bread + 80-100 g turkey + 250 mL fruit juice + a handful of Haribo sweets. Take creatine and digestive enzymes separately at the end of the meal.

OPTIONAL DINNER: 50-100 g white rice or rice cream + 10 g Parmesan. Take creatine and digestive enzymes separately at the end of the meal.

DURING THE FINAL 60 MINUTES BEFORE THE MATCH: 45-60 minutes before the match, take caffeine, 200-400 mg + theanine, 400 mg + L-citrulline, 6 g, or Beet-It, or a pre-workout nitric-oxide booster complex for a pump effect.
Remain hydrated by alternating plain water with water containing Reidrax, if any remains. Drink approximately 300 mL of fluid.


Five minutes before competing, take one final teaspoon of honey, approximately 10-20 g, or lemon-and-ginger Haribo sweets, or ginger sweets.

Plant foods during fight week:
During fight week, even with a very low fibre intake below 10 g/day, it is still possible to include small amounts of selected plant foods to add colour, micronutrients, and flavour without compromising the reduction in intestinal residue.
The aim is to choose low-fibre, easily digested vegetables and use them only as a garnish or as a small component of the meal.
Useful examples include:
Asparagus, 2-3 cooked tips


Spinach or kale, a few fresh or cooked leaves


Cherry tomatoes or cucumber


Peeled courgette/zucchini, cooked or grilled


Peeled bell pepper in small quantities


Pumpkin or pumpkin purée


Blueberries or strawberries


Avocado or guacamole


Parsley, basil, or coriander/cilantro for flavour


Chives, oregano, thyme, rosemary, or mint as herbs to vary taste and aroma


Fresh grated ginger or lemon zest to add freshness and support digestibility


Salt-free tomato passata or tomato sauce


These small additions help keep meals pleasant, colourful, and aromatic while respecting the need to minimise fibre intake and intestinal volume during the pre-competition phase.


After the weigh-in, nitrate-rich vegetables that may support aerobic performance include:

High nitrate content: beetroot, spinach, rocket/arugula, basil, celery, watercress, chervil, and lettuce.
Moderately high nitrate content: celeriac, Chinese cabbage, endive, fennel, kohlrabi, leek, and parsley.
Moderate nitrate content: cabbage, dill, turnip, and Savoy cabbage.
